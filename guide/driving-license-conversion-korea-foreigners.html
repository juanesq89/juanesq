Converting a Foreign Driver's License to a Korean License: The Full Process

By Juan, Attorney at Law 7 min read English

An international driving permit feels like a permanent solution, but it isn't — in Korea it only covers your first year of legal residency, and foreign license conversion (외국면허 국내면허 전환, oeguk myeonheo guknae myeonheo jeonhwan) is the step almost nobody explains to newly arrived foreigners until they're pulled over holding an expired permit.

Driving in Korea as a foreign resident runs on a deadline most people don't know exists. An international driving permit (IDP) or a foreign license used with a certified translation lets you drive legally for a limited window after you become a resident — after that, you're required to convert your foreign license (외국면허) into a Korean license (국내면허) to keep driving lawfully. The good news is that for most nationalities, the conversion process is far simpler than getting a Korean license from scratch.

→Why You Need to Convert: The One-Year Window

Once you register as a foreign resident in Korea, your ability to drive on an international driving permit or foreign license generally lasts only about one year from your date of entry or the start of your registered residency — not one year from whenever you happen to first drive. Many foreigners misunderstand this and assume the permit is valid for its full printed term, then get pulled over or stopped in an accident investigation only to learn they'd been driving unlicensed for months.

→Which Countries Get a Simplified Exchange

Korea maintains license reciprocity agreements (상호인정국가, sangho injeong gukga) with a large number of countries. Where your home country is on this list, the conversion process is streamlined — for most reciprocity countries you avoid the full practical driving test:

  • Full exemption countries — for license holders from certain reciprocity countries, conversion can be done with document review alone, no written or practical test required
  • Written-test-only countries — for others, you must pass a short written test on Korean traffic laws and signs, but skip the road test
  • Non-reciprocity countries — if your home country doesn't have an agreement with Korea, you generally must take both the written test and the full practical road test, similar to a first-time Korean applicant

Which category applies to you depends specifically on your license's issuing country, not your nationality — this matters for foreigners who hold a license from a country other than the one on their passport.

→The Document Checklist

Regardless of which category you fall into, the standard document set for foreign license conversion (외국면허 국내면허 전환) generally includes:

  1. Your original foreign driver's license, valid and unexpired
  2. A certified Korean translation of the license, or an accompanying international driving permit that serves the same purpose
  3. Proof of the license-issuing country's authenticity — sometimes requiring an apostille or consular authentication, particularly if the license was issued somewhere other than your country of citizenship
  4. Passport and alien registration card (외국인등록증) showing your registered residency dates in Korea
  5. A recent passport-style photo and a basic vision/health self-declaration or test at the test center
  6. Proof of overlapping stay in some cases — evidence you were physically present in the issuing country for a minimum period after the license was issued, to prevent last-minute license shopping before travel

The single most common reason a foreign license conversion (외국면허 국내면허 전환) application gets rejected on the first attempt isn't the test — it's a missing apostille or a name mismatch between the license and the passport.

→Where and How the Process Happens

  1. Go to a Driver's License Test Center (운전면허시험장, unjeonmyeonheo siheomjang) — the Korea Road Traffic Authority (도로교통공단) operates these regionally, and larger centers in Seoul and other major cities handle high volumes of foreign applications
  2. Submit your documents for review — this determines which of the three tracks (exemption, written test, or full test) applies to your license
  3. Take the written test if required, generally available in multiple languages including English at major centers
  4. Take the practical road test if required — this only applies to non-reciprocity countries or in specific flagged cases
  5. Pay the applicable fee and receive your Korean license, which is then valid on the same terms as any Korean-issued license, including for renewal

→Common Pitfalls

  • Expired foreign license — a lapsed license generally cannot be converted; you'd need to renew it in the issuing country first, which can be a real problem if you're no longer there
  • Missing apostille or authentication — some countries' licenses require formal authentication that takes weeks to obtain from abroad, so starting early matters
  • Name or date-of-birth mismatches between your passport and your foreign license — even small formatting differences can trigger a request for supplemental documentation
  • Assuming your IDP resets the clock — leaving and re-entering Korea briefly does not restart the one-year conversion window in the way some foreigners assume

→What Happens If You Drive Without Converting in Time

Driving past your window without converting is treated as driving without a valid license (무면허운전, mumyeonheo unjeon) — a real offense in Korea, not a paperwork technicality. Beyond fines, this materially worsens your position in the event of an accident: fault and insurance coverage both become more complicated when the driver is later found to have been operating without a currently valid license, and it can affect a foreigner's standing in any resulting civil or criminal proceeding.

→What to Do About Your Foreign License in Korea

  • Mark your one-year window from your registration date, not from your license's printed expiration or your IDP's term
  • Check whether your issuing country has a reciprocity agreement (상호인정국가) with Korea before assuming you need the full practical test
  • Gather documents — especially any apostille or consular authentication — well before your deadline, since these can take weeks to obtain from abroad
  • Double-check that your name and date of birth match exactly across your passport, alien registration card, and foreign license
  • If your license has already expired, or your conversion was rejected, get advice on your options before driving on an invalid license

Foreign license conversion (외국면허 국내면허 전환) is one of the more mechanical processes a foreign resident deals with in Korea, but the one-year deadline and document requirements catch enough people off guard that it's worth handling early, not the week your permit runs out.
